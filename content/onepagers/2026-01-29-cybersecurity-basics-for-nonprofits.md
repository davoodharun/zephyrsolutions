# Cybersecurity Basics for Nonprofits

## Risk/Impact
- Cyber threats can disrupt operations, compromise sensitive data, and damage your organization’s reputation.
- Nonprofits often handle donor information, volunteer data, and sensitive project details, making them attractive targets for cybercriminals.

## What Good Looks Like
- A strong cybersecurity posture includes basic protections like updated software, password management, and staff training.
- Regular backups of important data help ensure that you can recover quickly from a cyber incident.

## Recommended Next 30 Days
1. **Assess Current Practices**: Evaluate your current cybersecurity measures and identify potential weaknesses.
2. **Educate Staff**: Provide basic training on phishing, password safety, and data handling.
3. **Secure Systems**: Update software, use strong passwords, and enable two-factor authentication where possible.
4. **Backup Data**: Ensure important files are regularly backed up and stored securely.
5. **Develop a Response Plan**: Create a simple plan outlining steps to take in the event of a cyber incident.

## Cost Bands
- **$**: Basic training and free resources (online articles, webinars).
- **$$**: Low-cost software solutions, like antivirus and backup services.
- **$$$**: Hiring a consultant for a comprehensive cybersecurity assessment and tailored training.

Remember, taking small steps can significantly improve your organization’s cybersecurity while protecting your mission.
