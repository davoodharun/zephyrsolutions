# The Basics of Cybersecurity for Non-Profits

## Risk/Impact
- Non-profits face unique challenges, including limited budgets and resources.
- Cyber incidents can lead to loss of donor trust, financial loss, and disruption of services.
- Protecting sensitive data is crucial for maintaining the organizationâ€™s reputation and mission.

## What Good Looks Like
- A clear cybersecurity policy that all staff understand and follow.
- Regular training sessions for staff on safe online practices.
- Up-to-date software and systems to protect against vulnerabilities.
- A response plan in place for potential security incidents.

## Recommended Next 30 Days
- **Week 1:** Assess current cybersecurity practices and identify gaps.
- **Week 2:** Conduct staff training on basic online safety.
- **Week 3:** Review and update software and systems for necessary patches.
- **Week 4:** Create a simple incident response plan for potential breaches.

## Cost Bands
- **$ (Low):** Basic training sessions and software updates.
- **$$ (Medium):** Comprehensive training programs and improved security software.
- **$$$ (High):** Hiring cybersecurity consultants and implementing advanced security measures.

By taking these steps, we can better protect our organization and the communities we serve.
