Not long ago, a small nonprofit in our community fell victim to a phishing scam. An employee received an email that seemed to come from a trusted partner. They clicked a link, and before they knew it, sensitive donor information was compromised. 

The fallout was significant: not only did they lose trust among their supporters, but they also faced financial strain trying to recover from the breach. This story serves as a reminder that cyber threats are real, and they can happen to any organization, no matter the size. 

Taking proactive steps to protect your digital assets is essential. By staying informed and implementing basic security practices, you can help safeguard your nonprofit's mission. If you're interested, I'm happy to share tips to help strengthen your organization's defenses.
